## The Problem

Your best people can't be everywhere. Callbacks go unanswered. Voicemails pile up. Callers reach a generic bot that knows nothing specific. Or they reach a phone tree that frustrates them before they get help.

The real issue isn't that people object to AI answering the phone. It's that they object to being tricked by one that pretends to be human — or helped by one that doesn't actually know anything.

---

## The Solution

Echo Me Enterprise deploys an AI phone assistant built from a real employee's voice, knowledge, and communication style. It announces exactly what it is from the first word:

> *"Hello, this is an Echo of [Name]. I'm an AI assistant built from their knowledge and communication style. How can I help you?"*

Transparent. Trustworthy. And actually knowledgeable — because it's built from the real person.

---

## How It Works

**1. Build the Echo**
We capture the employee's voice (1-2 hours of recordings), upload their documents, FAQs, and knowledge base, and configure their communication style. The AI learns how they explain things, what they know deeply, and how they handle different situations.

**2. Connect to your phone system**
A simple call forwarding rule directs calls to the Echo. No IT project. No system replacement. Works with any existing phone system in about 10 minutes.

**3. The Echo answers**
Callers hear the employee's cloned voice. The Echo handles questions, finds connection points, and responds the way that person would — drawing on their actual knowledge and communication style.

**4. Smart escalation**
When something needs the real person: *"Let me have [Name] follow up with you directly."* The system logs the request and notifies the real person with a call summary.

**5. Continuous learning**
Every call transcript is reviewed. The Echo gets more accurate over time — learning new patterns, improving its responses, becoming progressively more like the real person.

## Multiple Echoes — The Knowledge Network

One Echo handles one person's call volume. A network of Echoes represents an entire department's knowledge — each one routing callers to the right expertise instantly.

When a company loses a senior employee, their Echo remains as a transition resource. Their institutional knowledge doesn't walk out the door.

---

## Employee Rights & Consent

- Every Echo requires explicit written consent from the employee
- Voice clone stays inside Echo Me — never exported or transferred
- When an employee leaves, company chooses: retire the Echo or negotiate a personal continuation arrangement directly with the former employee
- Employee can always claim their own Echo and transfer it to their personal echome.family account
